FPR-PFC — Capacity Assessment Questionnaire
FPR01 Station Operations · For internal reference only
FPR-PFC
1
Current Capacity Baseline
  • Q1
    How many active drivers does PFC currently have on the roster?
  • Q2
    What is the average number of packages assigned to each driver per day?
  • Q3
    What is the maximum package limit per driver per day?
  • Q4
    On average, how many hours does it take a driver to complete all deliveries? (Delivery time only, excluding pick-up and commute)
  • Q5
    How long does it typically take for a driver to complete scanning and pick up all packages at the station each morning?
  • Q6
    How long is the average commute for drivers to and from the station?
    This helps us understand the total daily working hours and capacity constraints.
  • Q7
    What is the current weekly check-in volume handled by PFC? What is the peak volume recorded so far?
2
Capacity Expansion Capability
  • Q8
    If the weekly volume increases from the current ~3,000 packages to 6,000 or 8,000, we estimate an additional 20-40 drivers would be required. Is PFC able to recruit and onboard the required number of drivers within two months of a confirmed volume increase?
  • Q9
    How long has PFC's current recruitment campaign been running, and how many drivers have been hired in total during this period?
  • Q10
    What is the driver retention rate? How many drivers have left, and how many of those have been replaced by new hires?
  • Q11
    In the event of a capacity shortage, does PFC have a backup pool of drivers available to cover the gap?
  • Q12
    What is the fastest turnaround time to bring in additional drivers if needed? Is there a contingency plan in place?
  • Q13
    Among the current driver roster, how many drivers have the willingness and physical capacity to handle a higher daily volume (200–250 packages per day)?
3
Route Management & Road Conditions
  • Q14
    How does PFC periodically assess which routes are under the most pressure? What is the process for rebalancing workloads across routes when pressure builds up?
  • Q15
    For routes with known systemic access barriers — such as gated communities and malls — does PFC maintain a structured record or database of gate codes and access information? If so, how is this information kept up to date and shared with drivers?
4
Driver Training & Daily Management
  • Q16
    What does the onboarding and training process look like for new drivers? How long does it typically take before a new driver is operating independently and efficiently?
  • Q17
    Are drivers fully aware of the key delivery metrics — specifically 2400 POD, 4800 POD, and Final Delivery Rate — and do they understand how their daily actions directly impact these numbers?
  • Q18
    What does PFC's daily driver management process look like? Is there a regular morning briefing or daily check-in with drivers before they head out?
  • Q19
    What is PFC's internal process for handling underperforming drivers? Please walk us through the steps — from first warning to termination — and how quickly each stage is triggered.
  • Q20
    When a route is under high volume pressure, is the station manager willing and able to conduct pre-scanning before drivers depart? Is this currently being done consistently?
    Note: PFC is currently required to pre-scan every day. We would like to understand the current status and commitment level.
